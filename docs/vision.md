# Vision

OpenInvestigator did not start as a software idea.

It started as a practical gap.

During my time in law enforcement, I worked on cases shaped by fragmented information, conflicting statements, unclear timelines, and too little structure for developing and comparing hypotheses in a systematic way.

One case in particular stayed with me. I spent a long time trying to reconstruct what had happened from incomplete and difficult material. There were mistakes in the investigation, but there were also broader limits: limited time, limited resources, and no real tool for making reasoning explicit, testable, and reviewable.

That experience shaped the core idea behind OpenInvestigator.

This project is not about replacing human judgment with automation. It is about supporting careful analysis when the material is messy, contradictory, and hard to reason about.

OpenInvestigator is being built around a simple belief:

Better tools for complex cases should not just store information.  
They should help people think through it — transparently, structurally, and from more than one plausible perspective.

That is the goal of this project.
